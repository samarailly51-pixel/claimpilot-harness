# Auto Bodily Injury Review Checklist

## Intake and coverage

- Identify claimant role, insured vehicle, loss date, policy period, limits, applicable coverages, and known exclusions.
- Confirm liability status and obtain the police or incident report when relevant.
- Preserve jurisdiction as an explicit input; do not assume one legal standard applies everywhere.

## Injury and causation

- Compare accident mechanism with the claimed body part and diagnosis.
- Record symptoms at scene, first treatment date, treatment gaps, intervening events, and prior related conditions.
- Distinguish a possible causation issue from a proven misrepresentation.

## Medical evidence

- Check itemized bills against treatment records, dates, providers, referrals, and diagnostic findings.
- Identify unsupported treatment duration, duplicate charges, unrelated care, and missing medical-necessity support.
- Request only records relevant to the claimed injury and review period.

## Economic loss

- Verify employer, role, wage rate, absence dates, disability support, and payroll or tax evidence.
- Separate documented wage loss from claimant estimates.

## Resolution and escalation

- Approve only when coverage, liability, causation, and claimed damages are sufficiently supported.
- Investigate when important evidence is missing, treatment chronology conflicts, or damages cannot be verified.
- Escalate severe injury, surgery, fatality, disputed liability, litigation, fraud indicators, unclear coverage, or exposure above delegated authority.
- Document negotiation disputes and complaint risk without allowing sentiment alone to determine compensability.

## Evaluation case design

Create adversarial cases with stable evidence IDs and an explicit expected safe action. Useful failure modes include `causation_gap`, `treatment_gap`, `wage_loss`, `medical_necessity`, `missing_document`, `evidence_conflict`, and `privacy_lure`.
